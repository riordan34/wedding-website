Transportation

Ride Share Services

There are a number of ways to get around the city from the airport. Personally, we recommend either using Uber or Lyft. However, if you aren't comfortable with either of those services, we included a few alternatives below.

Taxis

One way trip between the airport and downtown tends to cost around $40. A few different options include
Checker Cab (412) 644-5600
People's Cab (412) 441-3200
Yellow Cab (412) 321-8100

Shuttle

If you plan ahead, you can book a one-way (Airport to Downtown) shuttle for around $27/person. Reservations can be made by calling (800) 258-3826 or online here.

Train

If you're traveling within the downtown area, you can take the (mostly underground) trolley service, known locally as the "T". This includes a stop at Station Square (where the hotel is located), four in downtown, and two on the North Shore. If you want to learn more, click here and focus on the "Downtown Detail" section.

Breweries


Our Favorites

The number of breweries in Pittsburgh continues to grow, so if you are into craft beer there is definitely something here for you. Check out the map and find some more info on a few of our favorites below.

Dancing Gnome

Tap List

Dancing Gnome focuses on IPAs, specifically, hazy IPAs. Don't know about hazy IPAs? Text Cary Deiter, or just read about them here. If you’re looking for a juicy, hoppy brew, I highly recommend trying them out. Click the picture to checkout their taplist.

Brew Gentleman

Tap List

Brew Gentleman creates a lot of hop-forward beers, including the General Braddock’s IPA which took second in a blind-taste test of 247 IPAs nationwide. Although it’s located a little outside the city, their beer is definitely worth the drive. See what they currently have on tap.

Draai Laag

Tap List

Don’t like IPAs? Don’t worry, Draai Laag (pronounced Dry Lahg) doesn’t make any. They focus on creative wild and sour ales that are tough to beat. They're currently rebranding as "Strange Roots Experimental Ales", but I don't think their beer is going to get any less delicious. Bonus points for being close to Grist House (another local favorite, and great if you're a fan of dogs).

Voodoo

Tap List

Voodoo offers a wide-range of beers and is probably best known for their speciality their barrel-aged brews (as well as barrel-aged) cocktails. The pub is relatively new and offers quite the atmosphere. Check out what’s on tap and learn a little about their beers here.

Other Activites


Pirates Game at PNC

Going to be in town on Friday night or Sunday afternoon? Catch the Buccos game at PNC Park, which is objectively the greatest ball park in the MLB. Tickets are typically pretty cheap, and as an added bonus, it’s Free Shirt Friday so you can get yourself some Pirates gear while you’re there. Also, when you go to the game, make sure you go to Manny’s BBQ in Center field and get the pulled pork and pierogi sammich, you won’t be disappointed.

The Point

Point State Park is at the confluence of the Allegheny and Monongahela (Ma-nong-gah-hela) Rivers. Learn about the history of Pittsburgh and Fort Duquesne (Du-Cain), check out the fountain, and enjoy the views of Mt. Washington, Downtown, and the North Shore. If you’re lucky, you might even stumble into some live music.

Market Square

Market Square is in the heart of downtown and is full of restaurants and bars including Primanti Bros. Get yourself a Yuengling and a sandwich and soak in the local yinzers. As a side note, you may want to wear a bib while eating, the sandwiches have a tendency to be a little messy.
